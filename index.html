About My Research

I am an ecologist and evolutionary biologist with a focus on how species interactions, particularly predation, parasitism, and competition, shape adaptation and biodiversity. My research combines field observations, manipulative field experiments, and quantitative modeling to study eco-evolutionary dynamics in aquatic ecosystems.

Research Themes

  • Eco-evolutionary Dynamics of Host-Parasite Interactions - Investigating how parasites influence host behavior and ecosystem processes
  • Eco-evolutionary Drivers of Biodiversity and Coexistence - Understanding mechanisms that promote species coexistence
  • Experimental Aquatic Ecology - Using ecologically realistic experiments to test hypotheses
